05 · Editorial — The Slow Web, a newsletter

THE SLOW WEB · ISSUE 14 · 7 MIN

Why slow software feels fast

Naledi M · 4 July 2026

The fastest app I use all day is a plain HTML page that a friend never finished. It has no spinner because it has nothing to spin about: the page arrives whole, the numbers are already there, and the only thing left to do is read.

We spent a decade teaching people that software breathes — that a moment of skeleton screens is the price of anything happening at all. But the waiting was never physics. Most of it was ceremony: a bundle asking for another bundle, a client rebuilding what the server already knew.

A page that arrives whole is not retro. It is what fast always looked like.
from issue 14

None of this is an argument against ambition. It is an argument about where the ambition goes — into the words and the numbers, or into the machinery that delays them.
